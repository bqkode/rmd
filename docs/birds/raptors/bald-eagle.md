# Bald Eagle

*Haliaeetus leucocephalus*

The Bald Eagle is a powerful raptor and the national bird and symbol of the United States of America.

## Physical Description

- **Length**: 70-102 cm (28-40 inches)
- **Wingspan**: 1.8-2.3 m (5.9-7.5 feet)
- **Weight**: 3-6.3 kg (6.6-14 lbs)

### Distinctive Features

Adults are unmistakable with:
- White head and tail
- Dark brown body and wings
- Yellow eyes, bill, and feet

> **Fun Fact**: Bald Eagles aren't actually bald! The name comes from "piebald," meaning white-patched.

Juveniles are mostly brown with mottled white and take 4-5 years to achieve adult plumage.

## Habitat

Bald Eagles require:

1. Large bodies of water (lakes, rivers, coasts)
2. Abundant fish populations
3. Old-growth trees for nesting
4. Minimal human disturbance

## Diet

| Food Source | Percentage |
|-------------|------------|
| Fish | 56% |
| Birds | 28% |
| Mammals | 14% |
| Other | 2% |

### Hunting Techniques

- **Swooping**: Diving from perch to snatch fish near surface
- **Wading**: Walking in shallow water
- **Piracy**: Stealing prey from Ospreys and other birds
- **Scavenging**: Feeding on carrion, especially salmon

## Nesting

Bald Eagles build the largest nests of any North American bird:

```
Typical nest size:
- Width: 1.5-1.8 m (5-6 feet)
- Depth: 0.6-1.2 m (2-4 feet)
- Weight: 450+ kg (1,000+ lbs)

Record nest (Florida):
- Width: 2.9 m (9.5 feet)
- Depth: 6 m (20 feet)
- Weight: 2,700 kg (3 tons)
```

## Conservation Success Story

### Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1782 | Adopted as U.S. national symbol |
| 1940 | Bald Eagle Protection Act passed |
| 1963 | Population low: 417 breeding pairs |
| 1972 | DDT banned in United States |
| 1978 | Listed as Endangered |
| 2007 | Removed from Endangered Species List |
| 2020 | Population: 316,700+ individuals |

## Conservation Status

**Status**: Least Concern

One of conservation's greatest success stories. Population recovered dramatically after DDT ban and habitat protection.

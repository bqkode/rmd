# Northern Cardinal

*Cardinalis cardinalis*

The Northern Cardinal is a striking songbird known for its brilliant red plumage and distinctive crest.

## Physical Description

- **Length**: 21-23 cm (8.3-9.1 inches)
- **Wingspan**: 25-31 cm (9.8-12.2 inches)
- **Weight**: 42-48 g (1.5-1.7 oz)

### Sexual Dimorphism

| Feature | Male | Female |
|---------|------|--------|
| Body Color | Bright red | Warm brown with red tinges |
| Crest | Red | Red-brown |
| Mask | Black | Gray-black |
| Bill | Orange-red | Orange-red |

## Habitat

Cardinals prefer dense shrubby areas including:

- Woodland edges
- Overgrown fields
- Hedgerows
- Suburban gardens
- Swamps and streamside thickets

## Diet

Cardinals are primarily granivorous but have varied diets:

### Seeds (70%)
- Sunflower seeds
- Safflower seeds
- Corn
- Oats

### Insects (20%)
- Beetles
- Caterpillars
- Grasshoppers
- Snails

### Fruits (10%)
- Dogwood berries
- Wild grapes
- Mulberries

## Song

Both males and females sing, which is unusual among North American songbirds.

```
what-cheer, cheer, cheer
birdy-birdy-birdy
```

## Cultural Significance

The Northern Cardinal is the state bird of **seven** U.S. states:

1. Illinois
2. Indiana
3. Kentucky
4. North Carolina
5. Ohio
6. Virginia
7. West Virginia

## Conservation Status

**Status**: Least Concern

Population has expanded northward over the past century due to:
- Climate change
- Increased bird feeding
- Habitat modification

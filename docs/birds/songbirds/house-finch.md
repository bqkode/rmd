# House Finch

*Haemorhous mexicanus*

The House Finch is a small, social songbird with a cheerful warbling song, commonly found at backyard feeders.

## Physical Description

- **Length**: 12.5-15 cm (5-6 inches)
- **Wingspan**: 20-25 cm (8-10 inches)
- **Weight**: 16-27 g (0.6-1.0 oz)

### Coloration

Males display variable red coloration on their head, breast, and rump. The intensity depends on diet during moltâ€”carotenoid-rich foods produce brighter reds.

**Color variations in males:**
- Typical: Red to orange-red
- Some populations: Orange
- Rare: Yellow (carotenoid deficiency)

Females and juveniles are brown-streaked without red coloring.

## Habitat

Originally from western North America and Mexico, now found across the continent:

- Urban and suburban areas
- Desert scrub
- Open woodlands
- Chaparral

## Diet

House Finches are almost exclusively vegetarian:

| Food Type | Examples |
|-----------|----------|
| Seeds | Thistle, dandelion, sunflower |
| Buds | Tree buds in spring |
| Fruits | Cherries, apricots, strawberries |
| Berries | Mulberries, elderberries |

> **Note**: House Finches can be agricultural pests in fruit orchards.

## Nesting

House Finches are adaptable nesters, choosing sites like:

- Hanging planters
- Building ledges
- Conifer branches
- Old nests of other birds
- Traffic lights and signs

### Clutch Information

```
Eggs: 3-6 (typically 4-5)
Incubation: 12-14 days
Fledging: 12-19 days
Broods per year: 1-6
```

## Song

Males sing a long, jumbled warbling song lasting about 3 seconds. They sing throughout the year to defend territory and attract mates.

## Conservation Status

**Status**: Least Concern

Population underwent rapid expansion after 1940s introduction to eastern North America from pet trade releases on Long Island, New York.

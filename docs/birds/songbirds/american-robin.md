# American Robin

*Turdus migratorius*

The American Robin is one of North America's most familiar songbirds, recognized by its orange-red breast and cheerful song.

## Physical Description

- **Length**: 23-28 cm (9-11 inches)
- **Wingspan**: 31-41 cm (12-16 inches)
- **Weight**: 77-85 g (2.7-3.0 oz)

### Plumage

Males have darker heads and brighter breast coloration than females. Juveniles are spotted below and have pale spots on their backs.

## Habitat

American Robins thrive in a variety of habitats:

1. Suburban lawns and gardens
2. Forest edges and clearings
3. Agricultural fields
4. Mountain forests up to treeline

## Diet

Robins have a diverse diet that changes seasonally:

- **Spring/Summer**: Earthworms, insects, and larvae
- **Fall/Winter**: Berries and fruit

## Behavior

### Foraging

Robins are famous for their characteristic "run-and-stop" foraging behavior on lawns. They cock their heads to look for earthworm movement.

### Song

```
cheerily, cheer up, cheer up, cheerily, cheer up
```

Their song is often the first heard at dawn, earning them a place in the dawn chorus.

## Conservation Status

**Status**: Least Concern

Population is stable with an estimated 370 million individuals across North America.
